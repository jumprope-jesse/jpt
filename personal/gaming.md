# Gaming

Personal gaming interests and nostalgic games worth revisiting.

## Indie/Puzzle Games

### Sixty Four
- **Genre**: Idle clicker / factory simulation
- **Platforms**: Windows, Mac (Steam)
- **Price**: $6
- **URL**: https://store.steampowered.com/app/2471040/Sixty_Four/
- **What it is**: Start with one machine extracting black cubes, evolve into building intricate automated systems
- **Gameplay loop**:
  - Early: Active clicking to break cubes into smaller pieces
  - Mid: Build machines to automate extraction and processing
  - Late: Pure optimization and system-building
  - Can run in background but you won't want to look away
- **Tone**: Beautiful but disquieting - makes you feel like a Gilded Age oil baron
- **Key features**:
  - Mesmerizing percussive sound design
  - Sim City 3000-era micro-design aesthetic
  - Night mode toggle
  - Friend messages you while you obsess over machines (meta-narrative element)
  - Deeper mechanics beyond mining: "Hollow Fruit", fission, Hell Gems
- **Similar to**: Universal Paperclips, Factorio, Cookie Clicker
- **Notes**: Surprising depth for $6; reviewer got 8+ hours deep and still discovering new systems. Expect meta-narrative and themes about extraction/excess.

## Nintendo Switch

### Animal Well
- **Genre**: Puzzle/exploration (Metroidvania-style)
- **Developer**: Billy Basso (solo dev, ~7 years development)
- **Released**: May 9, 2024
- **Price**: $24.99 USD
- **Platforms**: PS5, Switch, Steam (PC)
- **What it is**: Surreal, atmospheric puzzle labyrinth with multiple layers of secrets
- **Inspirations**: Survival horror, adventure games, original Zelda and Metroid
- **Key features**:
  - Dense, interconnected world with puzzles that reveal more puzzles
  - Items have multiple uses for manipulating environment
  - Custom pixel art engine built specifically for the game
  - Designed for long-term discovery - secrets players will find "for years"
- **Tone**: Beautiful but unsettling creatures; dark atmosphere
- **Notes**: Highly acclaimed indie; no updates needed by design - meant to be complete and deep from the start
- **URL**: https://www.animalwell.net/

### Emio — The Smiling Man: Famicom Detective Club
- **Genre**: Murder mystery visual novel
- **Tone**: Dark, unsettling (among Nintendo's darkest games)
- **Gameplay**: Menu-driven with verbs (look, ask, review, think) - requires reading and repetition
- **Story**: Series of murders by a killer wearing a paper bag with a smiley face; connects to 18-year-old cold case and urban legend
- **Context**: First new Famicom Detective Club entry since late 1980s (created by Metroid designer Yoshio Sakamoto)
- **Similar Switch games**: Ace Attorney, Murder by Numbers, Coffee Talk
- **Notes**: Gameplay feels dated but core mystery is compelling; good portable "book-like" experience

## Gaming History & Engineering

### Starcraft: A History in Two Acts (The Digital Antiquarian)
- **URL**: https://www.filfre.net/2024/07/starcraft-a-history-in-two-acts/
- **Type**: Long-form historical article
- **Part 1 - The Game's Development**:
  - Blizzard's identity crisis after Warcraft II success (1996)
  - Early Starcraft mocked as "Orcs in Space" at E3 1996
  - Company pivot: cancelled most projects, focused on fewer high-quality games
  - "Asymmetric design" innovation: 3 truly unique factions (Terran, Zerg, Protoss)
  - Rob Pardo brought in as balance specialist - months of playtesting
  - Released March 1998, sold 10M+ copies, defining RTS of its era
- **Part 2 - Birth of Esports in South Korea**:
  - 1997 Asian financial crisis created environment for PC bangs (internet cafés)
  - Young unemployed men flocked to PC bangs; Starcraft became their obsession
  - Hwang Hyung Jun organized first TV broadcasts (1999), created OnGameNet
  - 2002 watershed: 20,000 fans packed Seoul arena for finals
  - Players became celebrities, earned hundreds of thousands annually
  - By 2004: 100,000 attended Starcraft final vs 30,000 for baseball
  - 2010 match-fixing scandal (Ma Mae-yoon) cast shadow over scene
  - Starcraft II (2010) disrupted the ecosystem; many moved to League of Legends
- **Key insights**:
  - Game balance crucial for esports viability (original Starcraft wasn't balanced enough pre-Brood War)
  - Cultural context matters: South Korea's crisis + new infrastructure + youth demographics
  - Professional gaming requires same sacrifice as traditional sports
  - "We don't really play for fun anymore. Mostly I play for work."
- **Documentary mentioned**: "State of Play" - chronicles South Korean Starcraft scene

## Family Gaming (Wirecutter Picks)

Games for playing with kids or family game nights, covering various ages and skill levels.

### Splatoon 3 (Switch, $60)
- **Genre**: Family-friendly third-person shooter
- **Gameplay**: Paint as much of the map as possible; play as squid-like Inklings that swim through paint
- **Multiplayer**: Team-based (4v4), with "drop-in" functionality for joining friends
- **Setting**: Post-mammal future world
- **Family bonding potential**: Excellent for distributed family hangouts over FaceTime/voice chat
- **Key feature**: No permanent death - players respawn when eliminated
- **Note**: Match setup can be awkward with 5 players (teams are 4) - one person needs to start a match, then others drop in for the next round
- **Real-world use case**: Great for staying connected with geographically scattered siblings/family through regular gaming sessions

### Cozy/Puzzle Games (Parent + Kid)

**Pikmin 4** (Switch, $60)
- Action-puzzle commanding cute plant creatures to solve problems
- Great for co-op play with kids
- Note: Subtitles required for alien dialogue; younger kids may need menu help

**Disney Dreamlight Valley** (Multi-platform, $40)
- Cozy game for Disney fans - gardening, fishing, rebuilding a village
- Solo-focused (multiplayer mode is limited)
- Good for Animal Crossing/Stardew Valley fans

### Party/Group Games

**Mario Party Superstars** (Switch, $60)
- Best boards/minigames from the series since the '90s
- Up to 4 players with basic controls anyone can learn
- Randomized events keep it fair for mixed skill levels
- Customizable rules and match length

**Nintendo Switch Sports** ($46-50)
- Motion-based: volleyball, soccer, golf, bowling, tennis, badminton, chambara
- Up to 4 players split-screen
- Works while sitting with wrist flicks (accessibility-friendly)
- Essential Switch family game

**Unspottable** (Multi-platform, $5-17)
- Hide-and-seek: blend into robot crowd, punch other players
- 2-4 players, rated E, simple controls
- Hilarious and unlikely to cause family fights

### Racing

**Dirt 5** (Multi-platform, $60)
- Split-screen racing for up to 4 players
- Easy controls, smooth handling, tons of cars/courses
- Available on Xbox Game Pass and EA Play
- Note: PlayStation requires all players complete tutorial first

### Cooperative

**Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes** (Multi-platform, $10-15)
- One player defuses bomb, others read manual on separate devices
- Great communication/teamwork game
- Works on VR, laptop, or console
- Can get heated as timer runs out

### VR

**Beat Saber** ($30)
- Slice blocks with lightsabers to music
- No skill required, great exercise
- Solo or take turns; Party Mode for multiple headsets

### Adults Only (After Kids' Bedtime)

**Jackbox Party Trilogy** ($60-75)
- Drawing, phrases, trivia minigames on mobile devices
- Up to 8-10 players depending on pack
- Standalone options: Drawful, Fibbage

## Classic RTS Revival

### Age of Empires 2: Definitive Edition
- **Platforms**: PC (Steam, Xbox Game Pass)
- **Status**: Actively maintained with ongoing expansions
- **What it is**: Remastered version of the 1999 classic, released 2019
- **Expansions since 2019**: 5+ including Return of Rome (2023) and Victors and Vanquished (2024)
- **Community**: Deep modding community; many official scenarios started as community campaigns (notably Filthydelphia's work)
- **Related titles in active development**:
  - Age of Mythology: Retold (full remake, not just remaster)
  - Age of Empires III: Definitive Edition (free-to-play with DLC)
  - Age of Empires IV (seasonal content updates)
- **Notes**: Remarkable longevity - 25+ years and still getting meaningful content. Good example of maintaining a classic while the RTS genre evolved into MOBAs.

## Classic Games (Modern Ports)

### Fallout Community Edition
- **URL**: https://github.com/alexbatalov/fallout1-ce
- **What**: Full re-implementation of original Fallout (1997) for modern systems
- **Platforms**: Windows, Linux, macOS, Android, iOS
- **Requirements**: Must own original game (GOG or Steam)
- **Features**:
  - Original gameplay preserved with engine bug fixes
  - Quality of life improvements
  - Configurable resolution (f1_res.ini)
  - Drop-in replacement for original executable
- **Also available**: Fallout 2 Community Edition (same author)
- **License**: Sustainable Use License
- **Notes**: Good for revisiting a classic RPG without DOSBox hassles
