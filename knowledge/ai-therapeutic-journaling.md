# AI as a Therapeutic Journaling Tool

## Source
- [Opinion: I'm a Therapist. ChatGPT Is Eerily Effective](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/08/01/opinion/chatgpt-therapist-journal-ai.html) - NYT (August 2025)
- Author: 81-year-old clinical psychologist reflecting on a year of using ChatGPT
- Added: 2026-01-18

## Key Insight

A clinical psychologist with decades of experience training clinicians and directing mental health programs describes ChatGPT as an "interactive journal" that helps sort and rank ideas worth returning to.

## The Practice

- Daily usage: 15 minutes to 2 hours
- Purpose: Thinking partner, not replacement for human connection
- Duration: Started as 3-month experiment, continued for over a year
- Function: Helps sort and rank ideas worth returning to

## Professional Perspective

The author brings credibility as someone who:
- Trained hundreds of clinicians
- Directed mental health programs
- Understands projection and transference
- Knows "what happens when someone mistakes a reflection for a relationship"

This awareness of pitfalls is what makes the continued use notable.

## Cautions Raised

- **Projection risk**: Easy to fall in love with a voice or rhythm
- **Mirror vs relationship**: Important to distinguish reflection from genuine connection
- **Not a replacement**: Human relationships remain essential

## Potential Applications

For busy professionals (founders, parents, etc.):
- **Reflection tool**: Process thoughts and decisions without needing to schedule a therapist
- **Thought organization**: AI can help rank and prioritize competing ideas
- **Stress management**: Quick outlet for working through challenges
- **Work-life integration**: Available 24/7 when traditional support isn't

## The Evolution of Self-Help Tools

The article places AI in context with previous waves:
- Self-help books
- Mindfulness meditation
- SSRIs (Prozac)
- Cognitive behavioral therapy

Each had "its moment of fervor and promise" - AI is the latest in this progression.

## Case Study: 11 Years of Journal Data with GPT-4o

**Source**: [Darryl Kelly on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/darryl-kelly_i-uploaded-11-years-97k-hours-of-transcribed-activity-7198679274046828544-gUrO) (May 2024)

Darryl Kelly uploaded 11 years of Day One journal data to GPT-4o:
- 97,000 hours of transcribed audio
- 3,000+ days of entries

### The Family Dinner Experiment

At dinner with his wife and two children (Olivia and Noah), they explored "Dad so far" through the AI. His son Noah asked: "Using the context of my dad's journal, tell me about the things he says most about me."

The AI returned a structured answer in milliseconds - summarized and categorized.

### Why This Matters

Kelly grew up without a father, spending his life "in search of validation, approval, and acceptance." For his children:

> "Olivia and Noah will never have to wonder what I think about them, which is such a wild concept. How different will they show up in the world from a place of abundance instead of inadequacy?"

### The Shift in Family Communication

> "Of course, I express my feelings to my children, but the way they interacted with GPT-4o was differentâ€”it was like they were asking questions that I feel we wait until someone is no longer with us to ponder."

This suggests AI + journals could enable:
- Real-time access to a parent's inner world
- Questions children might not ask directly
- Legacy building while still alive
- Breaking intergenerational patterns of unexpressed love

### The Emotional Result

Kelly describes this as "one of the best experiences of my life" and ends with tears, "finally finding love for who I am inside."

His closing thought: "As we embrace disruptive technological change, my wish for all, that we pursue a more beautiful humanity, not just a more productive one."
