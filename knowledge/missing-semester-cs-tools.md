# The Missing Semester of Your CS Education

Free MIT course teaching practical developer tools that CS programs typically skip.

*Source: https://missing.csail.mit.edu/ - Added: 2026-01-18*

## Why It Matters

- Students spend hundreds of hours using tools; thousands over a career
- Mastering these tools reduces friction and enables solving complex problems
- Addresses gap between academic CS and real-world engineering workflows

## Course Topics

1. **Course overview + the shell** - Bash basics
2. **Shell Tools and Scripting** - Automation, piping, scripting
3. **Editors (Vim)** - Modal editing, efficiency
4. **Data Wrangling** - sed, awk, regex, data transformation
5. **Command-line Environment** - Job control, tmux, dotfiles, SSH
6. **Version Control (Git)** - Beyond basics, data model understanding
7. **Debugging and Profiling** - printf debugging, debuggers, profilers
8. **Metaprogramming** - Build systems, dependency management, testing, CI
9. **Security and Cryptography** - Entropy, hashing, key management
10. **Potpourri** - Daemon processes, FUSE, backups, APIs, Markdown, Hammerspoon, etc.
