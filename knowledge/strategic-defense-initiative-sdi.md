## Overview

"Weapons in Space" by Aaron Bateman examines the Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI), Reagan's 1983 space-based missile defense program (derisively called "Star Wars"). Uses declassified American, European, and Soviet documents.

## Key Arguments

### SDI's Real Controversy

The central controversy around SDI was **not primarily technical feasibility** but rather tension over the role of military space technologies in American statecraft. This reframes the historical debate.

### International Phenomenon

Space militarization in the 1970s-80s was international, not just a US-Soviet affair. Western European countries participated in SDI research and development.

### Arms Control Impact

Even though SDI never materialized as a deployed system, it obstructed diplomatic efforts to create new arms control limits in space. The program's legacy continues to shape global discourse about space instability.

## Historical Context

After superpower detente fell apart in the 1970s, a new phase of space militarization began. SDI emerged from and accelerated this trend.

## Continuing Relevance

- Growing anxieties about 21st-century space arms race
- Framework for understanding current debates on military space technologies
- Tensions between technological advancement and arms control still active
