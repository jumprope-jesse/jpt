# Kevin Scott

**Role:** Chief Technology Officer, Microsoft
**Background:** One of Microsoft's key AI leaders, architect of the OpenAI partnership

## Key Facts

- Wrote book "Reprogramming the American Dream" (2020) about AI and the economy
  - Foreword written by JD Vance (now VP of the United States)
- Grew up in rural central Virginia - frequently references mom's healthcare access challenges
- Has a hobby Shopify storefront as an experiment to understand web business dynamics
- Hobbies include pottery (building a kiln) and making things
- No longer controls Microsoft's GPU budget (described it as "a horrible job")

## Views on AI (2025)

### Current State
- Describes AI as being in the "middle innings"
- Believes there's a "capability overhang" - models can do more than products enable
- Software development has clear product-market fit; everything else is lagging
- Honest admission: outside of software dev, doesn't delegate much to agents personally

### Scaling & Hardware
- Believes scaling law trends will continue but acknowledges difficulty
- Performance wins come more from software optimization than hardware improvements
- "Order of magnitude every year or so when you combine hardware + software optimization"
- Skeptical of AGI framing: "I don't even know what AGI is"

### The Agentic Web
- Key architect of Microsoft's "agentic web" vision
- Sees MCP as "the moral equivalent of HTTP for the agentic web"
- NLWeb = "HTML layer" for making websites agent-accessible
- Believes open protocols are technically inevitable

### Microsoft-OpenAI Relationship (2025)
- Relationship has evolved from "interdependent platform partner" to "big customer + platform-building partner"
- OpenAI still very important to Microsoft, but framing has shifted
- Still operates joint deployment safety board with OpenAI
- ChatGPT success "puts good pressure on Azure platform"

### Creative Economy & AI
- Doesn't use AI image generators personally
- Big Studio Ghibli/Miyazaki fan - wants creators protected
- Key distinction: AI for medical diagnostics vs AI for creative content
- Believes these debates shouldn't impede progress on high-utility applications

## Communication Style

- Thoughtful, willing to dive into weeds
- Admits uncertainty openly ("I don't know, honestly")
- Uses personal examples to ground abstract concepts
- References early internet era frequently when discussing new protocols
- Optimistic but realistic about what's working vs not
